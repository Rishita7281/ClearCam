Introduction About DCP Algorithm

About DCP Algorithm:

The Dark Channel Prior (DCP) is a concept and algorithm used in the field of computer vision and image processing, particularly in the context of single-image dehazing. It was introduced by Kaiming He, Jian Sun, and Xiaoou Tang in their 2009 paper titled "Single Image Haze Removal Using Dark Channel Prior." Here's a brief overview of the Dark Channel Prior algorithm:

1. Dark Channel Calculation:

- The dark channel of an input hazy image is computed by finding the minimum intensity value along each local window in the image. - Mathematically, for a pixel \(x\) in a local window \(W\), the dark channel value is calculated as: \(J_{dark}(x) = \min_{y \in W}(\min_{c} (I^c(y)))\), where \(I^c\) is the intensity of channel \(c\) at pixel \(y\).

2. Atmospheric Light Estimation:

- The dark channel is used to estimate the global atmospheric light in the scene. - The pixel with the highest intensity in the dark channel corresponds to the atmospheric light.

3. Transmission Map Calculation:

- The transmission map is computed using the estimated atmospheric light and the dark channel. - The transmission map \(t(x)\) is given by \(t(x) = 1 - \omega \times J_{dark}(x)\), where \(\omega\) is a small constant (usually set to a small value like 0.1 to avoid overestimation).

4. Haze-Free Image Reconstruction:

- The final haze-free image is then reconstructed using the estimated transmission map and atmospheric light. - The equation for reconstructing the scene radiance is \(J(x) = \frac{I(x) - A}{\max(t(x), t_0)} + A\), where \(I(x)\) is the hazy input image, \(A\) is the estimated atmospheric light, \(t(x)\) is the transmission map, and \(t_0\) is a small constant to avoid division by zero.
